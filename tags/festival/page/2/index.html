The Tomb Sweeping Day

  • Folklore
  • Festival , History

The Tomb Sweeping Day also called “Qing Ming” in Chinese has been a traditional Chinese practice for over 2,500 years and falls on the 15th day of the Spring Equinox, which usually falls on the 4th or 5th of April.

Water-Splashing Festival of the Dai People

  • Folklore
  • Festival , Legend , History

Water-Splashing Festival is the most ceremonious traditional festival of the Dai minority group. It usually takes place in mid-April of the solar calendar, often ten days before or after the Tomb-sweeping Day, and lasts three to seven days.
