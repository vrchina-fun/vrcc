Chinese Alcohol History and Culture

  • Culture
  • Food

In China, alcohol is also called the “Water of History”, because the stories of alcohol can be traced back to almost every period in Chinese history. A legend says that Yi Di, the wife of the first Dynasty’s emperor Yu invented the method to make alcohol In ancient China, since alcohol was regarded as a sacred liquid, only when people made sacrificial offerings to the Heaven and the Earth or ancestors was it used. After the Zhou Dynasty, alcohol was deemed as one of the Nine Rites and every dynasty put great emphasis on alcohol administration to set up special ministries to manage alcohol production and banqueting. Later, along with the development of zymotechnics and brewery, alcohol became an ordinary drink During the Tang Dynasty, alcohol was popular and was highly praised by many famous poets. It was served as the designated offerings for the Royal Ancestral Temple during the Yuan Dynasty.

In the beginning, millet was the main grain to make alcohol, thus the so-called “yellow wine”. Then the rice became more popular. It was not until the 19th century that distilled drinks become more popular. After the fermentation process, the Chinese alcohol has a balmy fragrance and is sweet without the taste of sharpness. Traditionally, Chinese distilled liquors are consumed together with food rather than drunk on its own.

Alcohol has a greater impact on Chinese artists than any other stuff Many of them produced their peak-of-perfection masterpieces when they were drunk. Many famous poets, such as Li Bai and Du Fu in the Tang Dynasty, had excellent performance and left us surprisingly marvelous poems after drinking.

Painting and calligraphy were raised to a higher level by the aid of alcohoL Wang Xizhi, Chinese famous calligrapher respectfully called Calligraphy Saint, retried dozens of times to overwhelm his most outstanding work - “Orchid Pavilion Prologue” which was finished when he was drunk, but ended in failure.

Alcohol has an internal connection with sociality in China. Drinking provides more chances for one to make more friends as the old saying goes, “Frequent drinking makes friends surrounding”. Moreover, alcohol also serves effectively to deepen and strengthen friendship. Alcohol is always used to relieve misunderstanding and hatred no matter how strong it is.

Chinese people do believe that moderate drinking of alcohol is good for health. When it is cold, drinking alcohol can keep out the cold and promote blood circulation. Many Chinese do sip a little alcoholic beverage at intervals to keep them fresh and healthy. Some even soak traditional Chinese medicine into liquor to achieve a better effect, which is proved to be successful.

Classification of Chinese Alcohol

Chinese alcohol can be generally classified into two types, namely yellow alcohol (Huangjiu) and clear alcohol (Baijiu). Chinese yellow alcohol is fermented alcohol that is brewed directly from grains such as rice or wheat. Both yellow alcohol and clear alcohol are traditionally pasteurized, aged, and filtered before their final bottling for sale to consumers. Yellow alcohol can also be distilled to produce white alcohol. White alcohol is also commonly called Shaojiu, which means “hot alcohol” or “burned alcohol”, either because of the burning sensation in the mouth during consumption or because of the heating required for distillation. Alcohol of this type typically contains more than 30% alcohol in volume since it has undergone distillation.

Da Hong Pao (Large Red Robe)

Da Hong Pao or Large Red Robe is the most famous tea in Mount Wuyi. It is a kind of oolong tea and less than 1kg of tea can be harvested each year.

Here is a legend about Da Hong Pao saying that a Ming Dynasty emperor’s mother was cured of an illness by drinking a certain tea. Then The emperor sent great red robes to drape on the four tea bushes to show his thanks. Now there are three original bushes still growing on the rock of Mount Wuyi which is said to be dated back to the Song Dynasty. The Da Hong Pao is picked from these bushes, so it is very expensive because of its rarity.
