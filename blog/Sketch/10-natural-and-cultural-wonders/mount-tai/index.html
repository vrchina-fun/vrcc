Mount Tai

  • Geography
  • Heritage , Nature

The leader of the “Five Sacred Mountains”, Mt. Tai is located in the center of Shandong Province, lying across the cities of Tai’an, Jinan, and Zibo. Its main peak, Jade Emperor Summit, which is within Tai’an City, is about 1,532.7 meters high.

The mountain was once called Mt. Daishan, Mt. Daizong or Mt. Taiyue and was renamed Mt. Taishan in the Spring and Autumn Period. The word “Tai” in Chinese means stability and peace, and the name Tai’an is attributed to the saying: “IfMount Tai is stable, so is the entire country.” Both characters of Tai’an have an independent meaning of stability and peace.

The formation of Mount Tai dates back to the Archeozoic Era, and now it is growing at the speed of 0.5 millimeters per year. It faces the Yellow Sea to the east and the Yellow River to the west and is in the neighborhood of Confucius’ hometown, Qufu, and the City of Springs, Jinan. It epitomizes splendid Chinese culture and was listed in the World Natural and Cultural Heritage List of UNESCO in 1987.

For thousands of years, Mount Tai has been the sacred mountain where Emperors held the ceremony of offering sacrifices to heaven and earth to pray and say thanks for peace and prosperity. In ancient times, the first thing for an emperor to do on ascending to the throne was to climb Mount Tai and pray to heaven and earth or their ancestors. It is said that seventy-two emperors of different dynasties have made pilgrimages here. Among them, Emperor Wu of the Han Dynasty visited Mount Tai eight times. These special ceremonies and sacrifices earned the mountain widespread fame. In addition, many poets and literary scholars also visited the mountain to gain inspiration and left numerous cultural and historic relics. The grandiose temples, the numerous stone inscriptions, and stone tablets remain today are the best testaments to these visits. Mt. Tai also plays an important role in the development of Buddhism and Taoism.

Taishan Mountain is a natural museum abounding with cultural relics and artworks. There are fifty-eight ancient building complexes, which are mainly in Ming and Qing Dynasty style. They are built at the foot of Mount Tai and along its slopes; among which Dai Temple, Bixia Temple, Hong Mountain Gate, Nantian Gate and Daizong Arch Gate are masterpieces among ancient Chinese buildings and the representation of China’s long and magnificent culture. Interestingly, from Dai Temple, located at the foot of Mount Tai, to Jade Emperor Summit, the temples built along the slopes are said to compose a ten-kilometer road from the nether world<?> to heaven. Stone carvings include the Buddhist Diamond Sutra in Jing Shi Valley, the Scripture of Mt. Tai and the Mo Ya Tablet.

Besides historic relics, this mountain boasts unique natural scenery too. The lofty peaks, deep valleys, spectacular waterfalls, enchanting rocks, and the centuries-old pines and cypresses will undoubtedly encourage you to linger around being reluctant to leave. The four wonders here are Sunrises from the East, the Sunset Glow, the Sea of Clouds and the Golden Belt along the Yellow River. It would be a great pity to miss the four wonders.

Hukou Waterfall

Located at the intersection of Shanxi Province and Shaanxi Province, is the second biggest waterfall in China. When the mighty Yellow River flows through mountains and gorges to Hukou, the billowy water streams narrow suddenly, falling 30 meters into a deep riverbed like a herd of galloping horses, transforming the quiet river into a turbulent one. The thundering sound can be heard from quite a distance. The tremendous mass of water strikes the rocks, creating piles of foam and huge water poles. It is an amazing view with mist all around. The riverbed here is like an enormous teapot absorbing all the rushing water, so the waterfall is named Hukou Waterfall, which means Kettle Spout Falls.

Baotu Spring

Baotu Spring is a culturally significant artesian karst spring located in the city of Jinan. Jinan is called “the hometown of springs”, with more than 100 springs. And among these springs, Baotu Spring is the most outstanding one and enjoys the reputation as “the No.1 spring in the world”. Baotu Spring has a history of thousands of years. It can be traced back to the Shang Dynasty in ancient China. The water of Baotu Spring spurts out in three prongs, with water droplets falling around, just like three piles of snow. The spring water is very sweet, optimum for making tea, which is limpid in color, rich in aroma and good in taste.
