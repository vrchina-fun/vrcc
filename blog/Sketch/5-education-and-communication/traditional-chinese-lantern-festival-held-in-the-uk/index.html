Traditional Chinese Lantern Festival Held in the UK

  • Culture
  • Festival

Traditional Chinese Lantern Festival is an emerging project in the events of “Happy Chinese New Year” in the UK in recent years. Since 2014, it has been alive in the UK. In these years, the festival has leaped into a special brand of the “Happy Chinese New Year” relying on rooting in the local and developed gorgeously. The festival not only shows the three major characteristics of “operation, marketing and participating by the mainstream” but also reach the win-win result of cultural exchanges and industrial cooperation of the “Happy Chinese New Year”.

The Traditional Chinese Lantern Festival debuted the famous UK Longleat House at the end of 2014. Since then, China has also held more than 10 activities covering major five cities in the UK such as the Chinese Lantern Festival in Longleat House, London Magical Lantern Festival, Manchester Lantern Festival, York Magical Lantern Festival, and Birmingham Magical Lantern Festival. Roughly millions of audiences watched and participated in the festival.

About 150,000 people watched the first Chinese Lantern Festival in Longleat House. According to the investigation, 97% of tourists expressed that this activity was brilliant. In 2015, the House held the second Chinese Lantern Festival, expanding to twenty-six exhibition halls from the basis of the last year with five added sceneries and nearly 23,000 lanterns as well as a great number of the tourists and revenue of the tickets increased. In 2016, the magical festival debuted in London with the duration time of one month and more than 100,000 tourists enjoyed it.

In the Spring Festival of 2017, the Chinese lantern festival appeared in the UK with richer form and more profound connotation, which gained popularity and praise from the local media and citizens. In order to celebrate the 50th anniversary of the opening of the wild animal park, Longleat House promoted the special session of Beatrix Potter with animal-shaped lanterns in the fairy tale, who is a famous English fairy tale writer as well as the writer of Peter Rabbit. The magical lantern festival was enlarged into three sessions in London, Birmingham, and York from last year’s one session only in London, which connected China’s lantern culture and British Christmas and the cultures of the above three cities, performing a more colorful magical effect. In particular, the event in London showed the splendid elements on cultural exchanges and business between China and the world along the Silk Road, with the theme of “Explore Silk Road and utilize the 3D and other technologies”. General Secretary Xi Jinping has pointed out in many occasions that we should strengthen our cultural confidence to push forward Chinese culture into the world, during which process we should firmly seize the basis and the soul of the brilliant traditional Chinese culture with five thousand years’ history, enhance cultural confidence and cultural conscience, pay attention to operating locally with innovation and abiding by the laws, which serves as a prerequisite and valuable experience.

China-South Africa Cultural Bond

The first annual “From University to the World” Student Dialogue was held on 1st September 2017, in Cape Town, South Africa. The event was hosted by China Radio International (CRI), China Plus and Global Max Media Group (GMMG). The event aims to promote cultural and social exchange between young adults from China and South Africa. With the steady trend of globalization, it is important for the future leaders of the two nations to build a relationship and have a better understanding of each other’s cultures. The establishment of friendship should not only be between the two governments, but also between the citizens as well. The participants of the dialogue were selected through online applications. After two rounds of preliminary judgments, the top 10 students will receive an invitation to attend the final event; of which 2 came directly from China, 3 are Chinese students studying in South Africa, and 5 are local students.

Beijing International Culture Tourism Festival

Beijing International Culture Tourism Festival, hosted by both the Beijing municipal government and the National Tourism Administration, is usually held from September 20 to 30 in Beijing annually. It is celebrated as “the carnival with Chinese characteristic festival”, with several foreign countries participating. This festival has been successfully held for many sessions. Beijing International Culture Tourism Festival boasts various kinds of cultural activities and grand ceremonies, including the dances with rich folk customs and full-dress parade which is the most famous performance in the festival. It has become a prosperous human landscape and an “urban calling card” of Beijing gradually, contributing to promoting international exchange and communication between China and other countries.
