The First Chinese Library in Mexico to Promote Cultural Exchange

  • Society
  • Education , Language

The first China-themed library in Mexico was inaugurated on Monday, April 24, 2017, in a bid to promote cultural exchange. Books on Chinese history, art, culture, traditional medicine and music, among other topics, as well as digital and video archives, can be found at the new Chinese Library at Mexico City’s Anahuac University. Officials from China and the prestigious private university were on hand to inaugurate the fourteenth library of its kind worldwide, as part of celebrations marking forty-five years of diplomatic ties between the two countries. The Chinese Library serves as a window that will allow Mexico’s different social circles to know Chinese better, and it will also be a new platform for deepening educational, academic and cultural exchange. China and Mexico can work together and continuously invest in the construction, administration and use of the Chinese Library, with the goal to tum it into a true cultural bridge that connects the two countries and a window that fosters bilateral friendship.

The library boasts 6,000 books and 80,000 digital archives with information on China’s politics, economy, culture, science and technology, and education and history. The 120-square-meter facility is equipped with large-screen monitors for viewing some 100 hours of video and virtual reality featuring China’s different regions, their ethnic groups, and lifestyles. Students of Chinese will find special software, in both Spanish and English, for learning Mandarin. Cipriano Sanchez Garcia, the rector of Anahuac University, said the library was made possible thanks to an initiative between China’s State Council and the center for higher learning. This collaboration proves human beings have the potential to approach one another, to learn from one another, to build bridges and to weave networks of cultural understanding.

The two countries share key traits. China and Mexico are countries with a very rich cultural heritage. Both countries are world-renowned for their uniqueness and have remained strong through constant innovation. Throughout history, people have seen how both civilizations have progressed and learned from one another. The library is bound to draw many visitors. The project would not have been possible without the support of the State Council, which helped immensely to make the library, which is full of knowledge and full of love, a reality. More material is set to arrive at the library to make classes more interactive. Since 2012, Chinese Libraries have opened in Japan, Tanzania, Congo, Nigeria, Uzbekistan, Australia, Belgium, Myanmar, Laos, Indonesia, Russia, Peru, and Turkey.

Exhibitions- a new highlight in China’s Cultural Exchange

Cultural relics exhibitions have become more important for China in improving cultural exchange. From 2011 to 2016, a total of two hundred and ninety-three exhibitions were held abroad to display Chinese cultural items, and one hundred and sixteen foreign relics exhibitions were held in China. One of the most recent exhibitions was held in February 2017 in San Francisco. A collaboration between the Asian Art Museum of San Francisco and China’s Nanjing Museum brought more than one hundred and sixty Chinese treasures, dating back thousands of years, to the United States for the first time. China has deepened cooperation with other countries in the field and she will hold more exhibitions abroad to present items that showcase Chinese culture.

Chinese Bridge

The “Chinese Bridge” Chinese Proficiency Competition is a large-scale international contest sponsored by Hanban and is well-known in the cultural and educational exchanges worldwide. It consists of three events: for foreign college students, foreign high school students and foreign students in China. Held yearly, this competition is now an important platform for international college students to learn the Chinese language and understand more about China. It also serves to help young people in the world better communicate with one another. Students who win the preliminary rounds held in their countries qualify to come to China for the semi-finals and finals. They will also get scholarships to further their studies in China as rewards.
