Hundred Schools of Thought

  • Culture
  • Philosophy , Personage , History

The Hundred Schools of Thought is the name given to philosophers and schools that flourished from 770 to 221 BC, an era of great cultural and intellectual expansion in China. Even though this period, known in its earlier part as the Spring and Autumn Period and the Warring States period in its latter part, was characterized by chaos and bloody battles, it is also known as the golden age of Chinese philosophy because a variety of thoughts and ideas were freely developed and discussed.

Life of Confucius

  • Culture
  • Philosophy , Personage , Education

Confucius has the greatest influence on Chinese characters. Besides being a great educationist, thinker, and unsuccessful politician, he was, first of all, an intellect with a noble morality. He pursued truth, kindness, and perfection throughout his life and his success and failure were largely due to his character, which had an everlasting impact on Chinese intellects.
